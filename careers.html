The Mission: Autonomous Operations for Manufacturing SMBs

Manufacturing SMBs are currently drowning in complexity. Every day, critical data is lost in a flood of emails, phone calls, and manual spreadsheets that try to bridge the gap between accounting, supply chain, and production.

Sophia builds the platform that ends this chaos. We don't just digitize processes; we automate them, driving immediate improvements in revenue and efficiency.

Why is this hard?

We are solving the problem of fully self-serve AI. While competitors rely on service teams to deploy their software, we are building a system where the software deploys itself. Our technical breakthrough is an interface where users correct and guide agents just as they would a junior employee — turning complex prompt engineering into natural, human feedback.

The destination is clear: Autonomous Manufacturing Operations.

About the Founder

I have spent the last 12 years operating at the intersection of manufacturing and technology. My path hasn't been linear — it ranges from co-founding Knusperreich (a pioneer in D2C food) to running a traditional family-owned precision casting firm serving the aerospace and medical sectors.

That deep dive into the 'Mittelstand' revealed massive friction in industrial procurement, inspiring me to launch KREATIZE. There, we helped machinery companies automate sourcing, backed by €20m in funding from Tier-1 VCs like Atlantic and Earlybird.

While I navigated the startup rollercoaster over the last decade, I often felt I was too early or too young to fully capitalize on major tech waves. With AI, that changes. I am convinced the next generation of value belongs to the application layer — solving unsexy, complex problems for real industries. That is the mission behind Sophia.

Operating Principles

1

Sequence Matters: We will not scale the organization until we have undeniable Product-Market Fit. We solve the pain point first; we scale second.

2

Talent Density: We hire fewer people, but better people. A small team of A-players outperforms a large army of average performers.

3

Speed is the Moat: We operate with extreme urgency to iterate, learn, and ship faster than the incumbents.

4

Builders Only: Everyone contributes directly to product or revenue. We avoid pure "management" layers entirely in the early stage.

5

Brand as Leverage: We invest in brand early to lower CAC and attract top talent, treating it as a compounding asset.
